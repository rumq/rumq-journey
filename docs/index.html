Brief Introduction : RUMQ Journey

Overview

This document gives a very brief overview of RUMQ Journey.

What is it?

It’s a program designed by Vikram Pawar, Founder, Servent, Director RUMQ LTD.

What does it do?

It helps job seekers whether they are freshers or career changers. It trains them to become exceptional International remote workers by becoming adept users of modern collaboration tools.

Why?

Traditionally freshers and career changers find it extremely difficult to secure jobs. They get exploited in the first few years by unscrupulous, money-minded entrepreneurs.

How does the RUMQ Journey help you?

The candidate benefits in the following ways.

IT industry worthy

They become IT industry worthy professionals even before joining any IT organisation. Typically freshers are not productive for at least an year in IT organisations. But RUMQ Journey graduates will be ready on Day 1 when they join the job.

What happens in the Journey ?

Typical life cycle of a RUMQ Journey graduate

  1. The candidate is introduced to Pawar and agrees to be on the Journey.
  2. The candidate joins a small team of 5-8 members typically managed by a Project Manager/Scrum Master & Pawar.
  3. This team operates as a typical Scrum team in an IT organisation. However, it will operate as one of the most efficient teams in the world.
  4. In a typical IT team, the whole team works on one product, feature or project. But in the RUMQ Journey team, each individual member works on their own set of tasks.
  5. Although each team member has their set of tasks, they collaborate with each other because
  1. They learn to work efficiently remotely.
  2. They learn Scrum together (Just enough to be managed effectively)
  3. They use common software that is most effective for fully remote teams. Most of these have been decided by Pawar for now. Suggestions are always welcome.
  4. They learn common good practices
  1. Not wasting other people’s time, and their time
  2. Having effective meetings
  3. Improving
  1. Reading
  2. Writing
  3. Collaborating
  4. Programming (if applicable)
  5. Project management (if applicable)
  6. Networking
  7. Helping others
  8. Working as a team
  9. Sharing rather than hoarding knowledge
  1. Each member prepares and maintains their own Backlog (ToDo list of all the items they want to get done). They’ll get guidance from Scrum Master and Pawar.
  2. They participate in a Sprint. A Sprint lasts for 2 weeks at a time, starts on a Monday and ends on a Saturday, and is typically 10 working days. However, the candidate can choose how many hours a day they plan to commit for RUMQ Journey. Even one hour should be enough, but has to be given consistently. At least some of the time has to be at the specified time of the day every work day.
  3. At the beginning of the Sprint, the candidate along with the Scrum Master(+- Pawar) decides which items in the Backlog should be prioritised and put in the current Sprint. This is typically done in a Planning Session.
  4. The Sprint starts on a Monday.
  5. It’s tracked using a sprint Tracker by the Scrum Master.
  6. The Sprint ends on the following Friday.
  7. Any tasks that are unfinished would be put back to Backlog along with other tasks to be reevaluated.
  8. This way with every passing Sprint the candidate would be moving towards the goal.
  9. The candidate will also be doing a Job Search on the side, and sharing the experience with the team, Scrum Master and Pawar.
  10. If the candidate secures a job, RUMQ will release the candidate.
  11. When Pawar is satisfied with the candidate, Pawar will recommend the candidate to his network.

How is it different from Training and placement?

Training and placement companies only teach technology. They charge money from the candidate to provide the training.They may also charge the company for placing the candidate. RUMQ won’t charge the candidate. RUMQ will also offer the worthy candidate to exceptional companies.

How is it different from companies that provide live project experience?

RUMQ doesn’t provide any live projects. All projects are selected or prepared by the candidates themselves.

Are there any expectations from those that want to be on RUMQ Journey?

Yes. The candidate has to be serious about learning to get better in their chosen areas. They have to be more active than a regular employee in a regular company. They have to maintain a commitment calendar (on Google Calendar) and inform of any absences well in advance. They cannot be absent for daily meetings or the training classes unless there is a good reason - a reason good enough for which they’d take an absence if they were working as an employee. We require this as this is essential to maintain the  motivation levels of those that are already on the Journey.
